PROJECTS

Here's a collection of some of my personal, hackathon and in-class projects sorted by the most recent.


December 2024 - "Ecouture"

Java | SQL

Ecouture is a sustainable fashion software project aimed at promoting eco-friendly wardrobe management and consumer awareness about the environmental impact of fashion, particularly fast fashion. It allows users to log their clothing items, providing detailed sustainability analytics based on material, brand, and acquisition method. With features like a closet database and a community forum, Ecouture helps young users make informed fashion choices.

April 2024 - "Study Space Finder"

HTML | CSS | JavaScript | SQL | PHP

This website enables users to discover ideal study locations by entering their zip code. It offers functionalities such as registration, login, listing pages with filters, and personalized dashboards that integrates with a live database.

November 2023 - "Sever Client Simulation"

Python

This program simulates a simple server client connection between the client, a local server, National Aeronautics and Space Administration's server, and National Science Foundation's server. The program showcases the steps taken to establish a connection between each server.

May 2022 - "Data File Manipulation"

C++

This program allows the user to display, sort, analyze, and manipulate information from a data file. Specifically, this program's data file contains information of Jojo Siwa and Jenna Johnson's scores for each week of "Dancing With the Stars" Season 30.

April 2021 - "BLISS"

Snap!

BLISS was created by a team of 3 aspiring women for a Women's Hackathon event and won 1st place. All art is original and drawn by the team. This game allows players to experience the environmental tolls of littering as they follow the journey of a ball of trash getting tossed onto the floor. From Seagull Betty to Gerald the Fish, players will understand the significance of their actions over the world and its many living beings, as one's misdeed of littering accumulates through the butterfly effect to harm many diffident creatures across ecosystems.

October 2020 - "Logit-19"

JavaScript

Logit-19 was created with 3 other team members for a Women's Hackathon event and won 3rd place. Logit-19 aims to address the mental health of teens during COVID-19, alleviating feelings of isolation. Through log books for recording emotions, fun activities to ease the mind, alongside tips and advice, it offers support. Its goal is to promote positive mental health and provide a platform for teens to express their emotions healthily.
