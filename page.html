PROJECTS

Here's a collection of some of my personal, hackathon and in-class projects sorted by the most recent.


Current Project - "Study Space Finder"

This work-in-progress website enables users to discover ideal study locations by entering their zip code. It offers functionalities such as registration, login, listing pages with filters, and personalized dashboards that will soon integrate with a live database.

November 2023 - "Sever Client Simulation"

This program simulates a simple server client connection between the client, a local server, National Aeronautics and Space Administration's server, and National Science Foundation's server. The program showcases the steps taken to establish a connection between each server.

May 2022 - "Data File Manipulation"

This program allows the user to display, sort, analyze, and manipulate information from a data file. Specifically, this program's data file contains information of Jojo Siwa and Jenna Johnson's scores for each week of "Dancing With the Stars" Season 30.

February 2022 - "Uploto"

Uploto is a patched-up prototype of a simple, conceptual social media app. It utilizes both front-end and back-end featues such as UI design and databases. This program can store login information for future use, some uploaded content, and reports.

September 2021 - "Doyun"

Doyun is a simple discord bot. You can ask it questions and request things from it as well. It is still in development and is in the works of getting back online. As for now, you can check out the code below.

April 2021 - "BLISS"

BLISS was created with 2 other team members for a Women's Hackathon event and won 1st place. All art is original and drawn by us. the environmental tolls of littering as you follow the journey of a ball of trash getting tossed onto the floor. From Seagull Betty to Gerald the Fish, experience the significance of your actions over the world and its many living beings, as one's misdeed of littering accumulates through the butterfly effect to harm many diffident creatures across ecosystems.

October 2020 - "Logit-19"

Logit-19 was created with 3 other team members for a Women's Hackathon event and won 3rd place. Logit-19 aims to address the mental health of teens during COVID-19, alleviating feelings of isolation. Through log books for recording emotions, fun activities to ease the mind, alongside tips and advice, it offers support. Its goal is to promote positive mental health and provide a platform for teens to express their emotions healthily.
